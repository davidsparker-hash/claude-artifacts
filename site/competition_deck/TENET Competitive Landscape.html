04 / 08

The honest answer to each — and why the gap holds.

01
↓ From above · monday.com & Make
Won’t a general work platform just add a production module?
The answer
They’re horizontal; TENET is vertical — and the vertical is the moat. Domain fluency isn’t a module you toggle on.
02
→ Studio side · Autodesk Flow, ftrack, NIM
Why won’t the entrenched tracker just extend?
The answer
Trackers show status to producers; TENET gives control to the client. None turn the money flow into a live client surface.
03
← Suite side · Adobe Firefly + Frame.io
Doesn’t a $280B suite with a workflow builder close the gap?
The answer
Adobe optimizes asset creation & review; TENET coordinates specialists and money. No cost engine on the project’s flow — and a credible acquirer.
04
↑ From below · Wrapbook & Saturation
Aren’t the finance tools one step from the pitch?
The answer
They track the money; they don’t carry the work between specialists. They never modeled the creative dependency graph.
The moatWhat survives every rebuttal
05 / 08

Two assets none of the four hold.

01

Bidirectional cross-vertical translation

Re-summarize one specialist’s work into the language the next one speaks — and auto-derive the cascading tasks when a change ripples up or down.

Trackers route · generators make · TENET translates
02

Client-side cost & timeline control

Put the budget-holder in the driver’s seat: dictate up or down, add or cut at the last minute, and watch cost and timeline recompute live.

The end of the change-order black box
The bet isn’t out-featuring any one rival. It’s owning the intersection of these two — the coordination layer — a different job than anyone above, below, or beside it is built to do.
The marketSize the layer, not the generators
06 / 08

The honest serviceable market is the coordination layer.

TAM~$255B
Global content production & coordination spend — the pool TENET rides on, not a serviceable target.
SAM~$4.9B
Production-management & coordination software for M&E by 2030 (~8.7% CAGR) — the layer TENET competes in.
SOM~$5.4M ARR
Near-term beachhead — JANE anchor + early enterprise. The seed base case at month 18.

Base case needs ~0.1% of the software layer.

A sliver of a ~$4.9B market growing ~9% a year — in which no player yet owns translation plus client cost control. The bet is owning the layer, not capturing the $255B economy beneath it.

Upside lever, not underwritten: per-project pricing rides the TAM, not the SAM.
