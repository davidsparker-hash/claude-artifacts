01

How TENET Makes Money

The value lands with the client — transparency, control, and the end of cost leakage — so willingness to pay is high and sits with the people who hold the budget. Revenue comes in three stacked streams, the same shape a proven peer (Magnific) scaled from ~$39/month individuals up to enterprise contracts — reaching ~$230M in recurring revenue, bootstrapped, with 1M+ subscribers and 250+ enterprise customers (BBC, Puma, Amazon Prime Video, the agency R/GA):

Revenue stream What it is Price (illustrative)
Anchor / enterprise Studios & production companies like JANE on platform contracts ~$20,000 / mo each
Self-serve seats Individual coordinators & small teams ~$79 / mo per seat
Usage / credits Compute for heavy AI translation & reconciliation ~25% on top of subscription

Because TENET sits directly on the project's money flow with full transparency, per-project or value-based pricing is also on the table — to be refined with early customers.

03

How Investors Get Their Capital Back

Read it like a development pro forma: put capital in, build the asset, the asset has an exit value, and the return is the stake ÷ the capital in. The asset's value is its recurring revenue times a market multiple (a fast-growing peer, ElevenLabs, trades around 22× revenue; a soberer coordination-layer comp, Wrapbook, raised at ~$750M on ~50% growth). The round is priced around a $19M post-money — the 2025 market standard for an AI-native seed — so investors take ~19.7% at close, diluting toward ~11% by exit.

Scenario Exit ARR Multiple Investor return
Conservative $3.0M 8× 0.7×
Base case $5.4M 15× 2.3×
Upside $10.0M 22× 6.4×

The point: priced at a defensible $19M post-money, the base case returns about 2.3×; clearing 3× and beyond comes from the upside (~6.4×) — a higher exit multiple or ARR above ~$6.9M. TENET needs only a sliver of the $230M category the market has already proven. The bet isn't that TENET becomes the whole market; it's that it owns the coordination layer above it.

06

The Structure

ANONA is the entity being funded. The seed capitalizes ANONA and the TENET build. Nothing in this round funds any other company.

JANE is a separate, related-party customer. JANE is ANONA's anchor customer, live test environment, and validation partner. Money flows one direction only — JANE pays ANONA, under an arms-length services agreement. Value never flows the other way.

The bridge is a subset of the round, not a separate plan. A light convertible note of ~$66K (three months of engagement plus legal and startup costs) funds the first 90 days and converts into the priced seed at close. It funds startup only — the full 18-month plan, team, and infrastructure are funded by the round itself.

07

Why This Fits a Real-Estate Lens

Recurring subscriptions behave like rent — predictable monthly income. Enterprise accounts are the anchor tenants. The Series A or acquisition is the refinance or sale that returns the capital. The build is the only speculative phase; after that, it's an income asset.
