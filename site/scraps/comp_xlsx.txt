WHY SIZE IT THIS WAY
The deck currently sizes off the generation market (Magnific’s ~$230M, the ~$175B AI-content TAM). That market is real, but it is the layer TENET sits ABOVE — the wrong serviceable market for a coordination tool.
The honest SAM is production-management & coordination software: the category Autodesk Flow, ftrack, NIM, Wrapbook and Saturation already sell into. 360iResearch sizes it at ~$4.9B by 2030, and its own function taxonomy lists Budgeting & Cost Tracking and Collaboration — exactly TENET’s scope.
TENET’s base case (~$5.4M ARR) is ~0.1% of that SAM. The argument to investors: we need a sliver of the software layer, not a slice of the $255B production economy — and the layer is growing ~9% annually with no player yet owning translation + client cost control.
Upside lever: per-project / value-based pricing sits on the TAM, not the SAM. Because TENET rides the project’s money flow, a small take-rate on coordinated spend reframes the ceiling well above the software SAM — the optionality the deck can flag without underwriting it.
Figures are market context as of June 2026 triangulated from third-party reports; private/forecast values are estimates. The ~$5.4M SOM reconciles to the ANONA seed model base case. Not investment advice.
