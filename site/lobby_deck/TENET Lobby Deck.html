Translation, not automation
05 / 12

You'd never summarize a building's wiring the way you'd summarize its interior.

The core isn't automating tasks. It's knowing how to summarize each specialist's work for the next — at the right level of detail, in the language that team actually speaks.

DEPARTMENT 01
Electrical
needs precision.
  • Load capacity
  • Circuit layout
  • Code compliance
"There will be outlets in every room" is uselessly vague here.
DEPARTMENT 02
Interior
needs feel.
  • Color & materials
  • Layout & light
  • The way a room reads
The size of the paint can is right for electrical, wrong for design.

Bidirectional · in real time
06 / 12

This is not a one-way pipeline. An interior designer reshapes a room, the client signs off — and the change ripples through electrical, structure, budget, and timeline.

Instead of grinding to a halt, TENET translates that change into exactly what each downstream team needs to do. It runs the other way too: a code conflict downstream ripples back up in the language the designer needs.

Managing the cascading implications of change across parallel teams is what separates a workflow system from a task tracker.
