The Translation Layer Translation, not automation
05 / 22

It's translation, not automation.

TENET carries work between specialists who speak different professional languages — accurately, in real time, in both directions — without losing detail, time, or money in the gaps.

It is not a tool that makes the work. It is the system that carries the work correctly between the people and tools that do.

01 · Summarize correctly
Speak every language

Know the language each department speaks — electrical needs load and code; interior needs color and feel — and summarize each for the next at the right level of detail.

02 · Bidirectional
Ripples both ways

A change upstream ripples into exactly the tasks each downstream team needs. A conflict downstream ripples back up, in the language that team needs.

03 · Client control
Cost, live

The budget-holder dictates up or down and watches cost and timeline recompute live. Opacity replaced with mathematics.

The Honest View The risks — and how we hold them
20 / 22

The risks — and how we hold them.

Risk 01
The layer is contested

Four well-funded categories are converging on the coordination layer from four directions at once.

How we hold it

None holds translation + client cost control — we lead with that intersection, not an “empty field” claim.

Risk 02
Multiple-compression

An investor anchored on audio-AI multiples (ElevenLabs ~25–33×) will pressure our exit.

How we hold it

We anchor instead on the soberer coordination comp — Wrapbook ~$750M on ~50% growth — and run 8× / 15× / 22×.

Risk 03
The 3× gap

At a $19M post-money the base case returns ~2.3×; clearing 3× leans on the upside.

How we hold it

We state it openly — ARR above ~$6.9M or a higher multiple gets there, and we price for it.

Risk 04
Single anchor customer

JANE is one customer today, which concentrates early revenue.

How we hold it

The pilot-to-platform motion and the self-serve seat ramp de-risk concentration across the 18 months.
