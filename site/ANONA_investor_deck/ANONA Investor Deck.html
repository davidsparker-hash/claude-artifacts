Generative AI made it dramatically easier to produce beautiful images, video, and content. But when creation becomes easy, something else becomes scarce: structure, judgment, and direction.

The Shift In Real Numbers Three Cisco campaigns · three years
04 / 26

Three Cisco campaigns. Three consecutive years. One story.

Source · JANE · full CG → full AI production

Across three consecutive years — full CG in year one, hybrid in year two, full AI most recently — hard costs dropped from 62 cents on the dollar to 23 cents. Margins nearly doubled, and held at scale.

This is not a projection. Not a model. This is what happened.

AI production has opened a margin window. But windows close — clients already know generation is cheap. The next advantage won't be production efficiency. It will be creative conviction: knowing which idea to pursue before abundance becomes paralysis.

That is the problem TENET solves.

06 — The flywheel
Introducing TENET Script in by Monday · approved by Friday
07 / 26

A script becomes a shared visual narrative — while change is still free.

Picture this: your team uploads a script Monday morning; by lunch they're reviewing five different edits. By Friday the creative is client-approved, the shoot is scheduled, and the shots that can be fully AI are being developed in parallel.

Not because AI is magic — but because we made the early ideation loop fast for creatives who typically spend 70% of their time on logistics instead of story.

TENET is not a production tool. It is a decision-making environment for early creative development.

Teams can
  • Upload a script or outline
  • Generate a timed narrative sequence
  • Refine scenes collaboratively
  • Adjust tone, structure, and direction in one shared environment

TENET lives upstream of execution tools. It is not designed to create final assets — it is designed to structure the decision of what deserves to be created.

13 — The compounding advantage
Why I'm Building It I've lived this problem
14 / 26

I've lived this problem.

For twenty-six years I've worked inside advertising and post-production — building companies and helping bring creative work to life. Across hundreds of campaigns and productions, I kept seeing the same thing happen.

Not because people lacked talent, but because clarity arrived too late. By the time everyone could actually see the idea clearly, momentum and budget were already pushing the project forward.

Stronger ideas often appeared after it was too costly to pivot — or were killed early because the client couldn't see them yet and called them a gamble.

After years of watching that happen, I started building the tool I wished teams had at the beginning of the process.

ANONA builds that environment. TENET is the first product.
